1. What Information Do We Collect?

Personal information you disclose to us

In short: We collect a small amount of personal information that you voluntarily provide and that is stored locally on your device.

The personal information we collect depends on the context of your interactions with us and the Services, the choices you make, and the products and features you use. Personal information you may provide includes:

  • First name or display name — used to personalize the app experience (e.g., "Good morning, Ibrahim"). You can enter any name you choose, including a nickname or pseudonym. This is stored locally on your device only.

Sensitive Information. We do not process sensitive information.

Payment Data. If you choose to subscribe, payment information is collected and processed entirely by Apple Inc. through the App Store and managed via RevenueCat. We never see, access, or store your payment card information. Subscription status (whether you have an active subscription, the tier, and renewal dates) is provided to ScreenReps in real-time by these services to gate premium features. You can review their privacy notices here:

  • Apple: https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/
  • RevenueCat: https://www.revenuecat.com/privacy/

Application Data. If you use ScreenReps, we may also process the following information based on permissions you grant in iOS:

  • Geolocation Information. If you choose to use the gym check-in feature, we request access to your location only at the moment you tap "check in." Your coordinates are compared on-device to your user-pinned gym location, then immediately discarded. No coordinate history is stored. You can change permissions any time in iOS Settings.
  • Camera Access. For pushup and squat exercise tracking, we request camera access. Camera frames are processed in real-time on your device using Apple's Vision framework to detect body keypoints for rep counting. No images, video, or visual data are saved, recorded, or transmitted. The camera is only active when you start an exercise session.
  • Health Data. For walking-based exercise tracking, we request read-only access to step count via Apple HealthKit. Step counts are read on-device and used to calculate earned screen time. No HealthKit data is transmitted off your device.
  • Screen Time / Family Controls. We use Apple's Family Controls and Screen Time APIs to apply app shields and track usage of apps you have selected to block. All Screen Time data remains on your device and is processed by iOS — we never receive or transmit your app usage history.
  • Push Notifications. We may send local push notifications for exercise reminders, time bank status, and similar operational messages. You can opt out at any time in iOS Settings.

This information is primarily needed to maintain the security and operation of the application and to provide the features you've enabled.

All personal information that you provide to us must be true, complete, and accurate, and you must notify us of any changes to such personal information.

5. Do We Offer Artificial Intelligence-Based Products?

In short: Yes. ScreenReps uses on-device machine learning (Apple's Vision framework) to count exercise repetitions from camera frames. All AI processing happens entirely on your device — nothing is transmitted off-device.

As part of our Services, we offer features powered by machine learning, specifically Apple's Vision framework for body pose detection. The terms in this Privacy Notice govern your use of these features.

Our AI Products

Our AI products are designed for the following functions:

  • Image analysis (analyzing camera frames in real-time to detect body keypoints)
  • Machine learning models (Apple's pre-trained pose detection model, running entirely on-device)

How We Process Your Data Using AI

When you use camera-based exercise tracking (pushups or squats), camera frames are processed in real-time on your device using Apple's Vision framework. Each frame is analyzed and immediately discarded. No images, video, or any visual data is saved, recorded, or transmitted off your device. We never see what your camera sees — only the resulting rep counts are retained.

How to Opt Out

We believe in giving you full control over how your data is used. To opt out of camera-based AI processing, you can:

  • Choose non-camera exercises instead — walking (HealthKit), plank (timer-based), or gym check-in (location-based) — none of which use AI
  • Decline camera permission when prompted, or revoke it at any time in iOS Settings → ScreenReps → Camera
  • Contact us at privacy@scarabyte.app with any questions

7. How Long Do We Keep Your Information?

In short: Most of your data is stored only on your device for as long as you have ScreenReps installed. We do not maintain server-side copies of your data.

Because ScreenReps stores your data locally on your device:

  • Personal information (display name), exercise data, time bank, and settings: Retained on your device until you delete the app or clear the data within ScreenReps. Uninstalling the app removes all of this information.
  • Camera frames (for AI rep detection): Not retained. Frames are processed in real-time and discarded immediately.
  • Geolocation (for gym check-in): Not retained. Coordinates are compared on-device and discarded immediately. Only the result of the check-in (timestamp, gym ID, success/failure) is retained on your device.
  • Subscription data: Managed by Apple and RevenueCat per their respective retention policies. ScreenReps does not maintain its own copy.

When we have no ongoing legitimate business need to process your personal information, we will either delete or anonymize such information.

10. Controls For Do-Not-Track Features

Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems and mobile applications include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT") feature or setting you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected. At this stage, no uniform technology standard for recognizing and implementing DNT signals has been finalized. As such, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online.

California law requires us to let you know how we respond to web browser DNT signals. Because there currently is not an industry or legal standard for recognizing or honoring DNT signals, we do not respond to them at this time.
